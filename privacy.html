4. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your browsing experience and to gather data about how our website is used. Here’s a closer look at how these technologies work:

4.1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help the site remember your preferences and provide a more personalized experience by storing information about your visit.

4.2. Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential Cookies: These are necessary for the functioning of our website and enable you to navigate and use its features.
  • Performance Cookies: These collect information about how visitors use our site, helping us improve its performance and user experience.
  • Functional Cookies: These allow the website to remember your preferences and settings to provide a more personalized experience.
  • Targeting Cookies: These are used to deliver targeted advertising based on your interests and browsing history.

4.3. Managing Cookies

You can control and manage cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to refuse or delete cookies. However, if you disable cookies, some features of our website may not function properly.

4.4. Tracking Technologies

In addition to cookies, we use other tracking technologies such as web beacons and pixels to monitor user activity and collect data about website usage. These technologies help us understand user behavior and improve our services.

5.1. Security Measures

  • Access Controls: We limit access to personal information to authorized personnel who need it for their job functions.
  • Regular Audits: We conduct regular security audits and vulnerability assessments to identify and address potential weaknesses.
